Simplicity requirements:

Vague uncertainty requirements:
Use less technical uncertainty language, such as:
- "Your doctors are not completely sure yet."
- "They want to check this again."

- remove major uncertainty,
- turn uncertain findings into confirmed facts,
- say something is probably harmless unless the fact base says that,
- give false reassurance,
- say "you do not need to worry,"
- say the treatment will definitely work.
