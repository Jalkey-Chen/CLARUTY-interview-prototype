# Trust-building Script

## Prototype note

This is placeholder script content for the CLARITY interview prototype. It uses only the shared fact base and should be reviewed by a clinician before patient-facing use.

## Speaker 1 / Speaker 2 dialogue

### Opening

**Speaker 1:** Medical notes can feel dense, but the main points can be explained clearly.

**Speaker 2:** This walkthrough is meant to support understanding and help a patient prepare for conversations with their care team.

### Main diagnosis

**Speaker 1:** The note lists the diagnosis as metastatic thymic carcinoma.

**Speaker 2:** That means the cancer started in the thymus area and has spread beyond that area.

### What the scans and biopsy showed

**Speaker 1:** The note describes a mass in the anterior mediastinum, near the thymus area.

**Speaker 2:** It also describes involvement of the liver, lymph nodes, bones, small lung nodules, and possible very small brain findings that need follow-up.

### Current and recommended treatment

**Speaker 1:** The patient has already started carboplatin and paclitaxel while inpatient.

**Speaker 2:** The note recommends adding ramucirumab to carboplatin and paclitaxel, with repeat imaging after the first two cycles if clinically appropriate.

### What is still uncertain

**Speaker 1:** The note is careful about the tiny brain MRI findings. They may be vascular structures or tiny metastases, so follow-up imaging is important.

**Speaker 2:** The care team may also monitor the mildly positive ANA and low reticulocytes because they could matter for future treatment planning or blood-related monitoring.

### Questions to discuss with the care team

**Speaker 1:** It is reasonable to ask about treatment goals, side effects, and timing for repeat imaging.

**Speaker 2:** It is also reasonable to ask what symptoms should prompt contact with the care team.

### Closing

**Speaker 1:** Understanding the note can help a patient participate in care conversations.

**Speaker 2:** This prototype should be reviewed by clinicians before real patient-facing use.
