# Technical + Explicit Uncertainty Script

## Prototype note

This is placeholder script content for the CLARITY interview prototype. It preserves more clinical terminology from the shared fact base and should be reviewed by a clinician before patient-facing use.

## Speaker 1 / Speaker 2 dialogue

### Opening

**Speaker 1:** This version keeps more of the clinical language from the note while still explaining the main points.

**Speaker 2:** It also states uncertainty explicitly, especially where the note calls for monitoring or follow-up.

### Main diagnosis

**Speaker 1:** The diagnosis in the note is metastatic thymic carcinoma, staged as IVB.

**Speaker 2:** Stage IVB indicates disease beyond the thymus region, consistent with the metastatic sites described in the note.

### What the scans and biopsy showed

**Speaker 1:** The fact base lists an anterior mediastinal mass in the thymus area, with disease involving the liver, lymph nodes, bones, and small lung nodules.

**Speaker 2:** It also lists possible very small brain findings that require follow-up rather than immediate overinterpretation.

### Current and recommended treatment

**Speaker 1:** Carboplatin and paclitaxel were started inpatient.

**Speaker 2:** The recommended next treatment is carboplatin, paclitaxel, and ramucirumab, with repeat imaging after the first two cycles if clinically appropriate.

### What is still uncertain

**Speaker 1:** The tiny brain MRI findings may represent vascular structures or tiny metastases. The note does not resolve that distinction.

**Speaker 2:** A mildly positive ANA may affect future immunotherapy considerations, and low reticulocytes may require monitoring for possible early pure red cell aplasia.

### Questions to discuss with the care team

**Speaker 1:** Ask about the goal of therapy, expected toxicities, and when repeat imaging will happen.

**Speaker 2:** Also ask how the uncertain MRI findings, ANA result, and reticulocyte finding affect monitoring or treatment planning.

### Closing

**Speaker 1:** This is a technical prototype explanation, not medical advice.

**Speaker 2:** Final content should be checked against the clinical note and reviewed by clinicians before use.
