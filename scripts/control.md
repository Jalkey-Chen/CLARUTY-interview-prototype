# Balanced / Control Script

## Prototype note

This is placeholder script content for the CLARITY interview prototype. It uses only the shared fact base and should be reviewed by a clinician before patient-facing use.

## Speaker 1 / Speaker 2 dialogue

### Opening

**Speaker 1:** Today we will walk through the main points from this de-identified clinical note in plain language.

**Speaker 2:** The goal is to help a patient understand what the note says, what treatment has started, and what questions may be useful for the care team.

### Main diagnosis

**Speaker 1:** The diagnosis listed in the note is metastatic thymic carcinoma.

**Speaker 2:** Metastatic means the cancer has spread beyond the thymus area.

### What the scans and biopsy showed

**Speaker 1:** The note describes an anterior mediastinal mass in the thymus area, with involvement of the liver, lymph nodes, bones, small lung nodules, and possible very small brain findings that need follow-up.

**Speaker 2:** The biopsy information in the note supports thymic carcinoma.

### Current and recommended treatment

**Speaker 1:** Treatment has started with carboplatin and paclitaxel while inpatient.

**Speaker 2:** The note recommends carboplatin, paclitaxel, and ramucirumab, with repeat imaging after the first two cycles if clinically appropriate.

### What is still uncertain

**Speaker 1:** Some very small brain MRI findings may be blood vessels or tiny metastases, so short-term follow-up imaging is needed.

**Speaker 2:** The note also mentions a mildly positive ANA and low reticulocytes as points the care team may monitor.

### Questions to discuss with the care team

**Speaker 1:** Useful questions include: what is the goal of treatment, what side effects should I watch for, and when will repeat imaging happen?

**Speaker 2:** It may also help to ask what the uncertain brain MRI findings mean and which symptoms should prompt a call to the care team.

### Closing

**Speaker 1:** This explainer is a prototype based on a de-identified note.

**Speaker 2:** Final content should be reviewed by a clinician before use.
